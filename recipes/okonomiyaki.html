Okonomiyaki (“Japanese Pizza”)

Description

Often dubbed as “Japanese pizza” or a “Japanese savory pancake,” okonomiyaki is a highly customizable dish that comes in a variety of styles and combines a savory batter with various fillings and toppings—as the name suggests, it's what you like, grilled. Here is a recipe for an Osaka-style okonomiyaki.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cabbage, finely shredded
  • 1 cup leeks, well-washed and chopped
    • Recommendation: slice them lengthwise, then submerge and wash them in a bowl of water before slicing.
  • A few pinches of fine grain sea salt
  • 2/3 cup whole wheat pastry flour
    • Alternative: 2/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1+ tbsp olive oil
  • Garnish (toasted slivered almonds, chives/any herbs you like)

Directions

  1. Combine the cabbage, leeks, flour, and salt in a bowl. Toss until everything is coated with a dusting of flour. Stir in the eggs and mix until everything is evenly coated.
  2. Heat a large skillet over medium heat and add a generous splash of olive oil.
  3. Scoop the cabbage mixture into the pan and press it into a round pancake shape using a metal spatula, as flat as you can get it.
  4. Cook for 4-5 minutes, or until the bottom is golden.
  5. Flip the okonomiyaki—slide it out of the skillet onto a plate, plate another plate on top, and flip both over at the same time.
    • If you need more oil in your skillet, add the oil before sliding the okonomiyaki back into the skillet.
  6. Press down on the okonomiyaki with a spatula and cook until golden (≈3-5 minutes).
  7. Garnish, then cut into wedges.
