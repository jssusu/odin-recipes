Mochi

Description

Mochi (moh-chee) is a popular bun-shaped Japanese dessert made from sweet glutinous rice flour (mochigome) with a soft, chewy, and somewhat elastic texture.
Mochi is often flavored with green tea (matcha) powder, giving it a light green hue, but this recipe uses red bean paste (adzuki bean paste/anko) in addition with matcha to provide a unique flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sweetened red bean paste (adzuki bean paste/anko)
  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour (mochigome)
  • 1 tsp green tea powder (matcha)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch (to roll out the dough)

Directions

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Wrap red bean paste in aluminum foil and place in the freezer until solid (≥1 hour).
  3. Mix glutinous rice flour and green tea powder thoroughly in a microwave-safe bowl.
  4. Stir in water, then sugar. Mix until the mixture is smooth.
  5. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and microwave for 3 minutes and 30 seconds.
  6. While the rice flour mixture is in the microwave, remove the red bean paste from the freezer and divide it into 8 equal balls. Set these aside for later (step 8).
  7. Remove the rice flour mixture from the microwave. Stir and heat for another 15-30 seconds, covered.
  8. Dust a work surface with cornstarch.
  9. Roll about 2 tablespoons of hot rice flour mixture in a ball, flatten it, and place a ball of frozen red bean paste in the center.
  10. Pinch and press the dough around the bean paste until it is completely covered.
  11. Sprinkle the mochi with additional cornstarch and place seam-side down in a paper muffin liner to prevent sticking.
  12. Repeat steps 9-11 to make additional mochi with remaining ingredients, redusting your work surface with cornstarch when necessary.
